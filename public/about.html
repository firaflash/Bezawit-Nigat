Years

of working experience as a architect and visual artist

Starting from my mother and women in general, it is fascinating how they hide their pain and sorrow in makeup and artificial material. My art explores the contrast between external beauty and inner truth—revealing what lies beneath the surface.

I create to tell stories—of joy, resilience, ancestral wisdom, and the cosmic journey of self-discovery. Whether through murals, live performances, or conceptual paintings, my work blends Ethiopian heritage with universal human experiences.

Original Acrylic & Mural Art

Live Art Performances

Commissioned Conceptual Works

My works include large-scale murals like the one at the France Embassy (on Liberty, Fraternity, and Equality), live performances for Wegene Charity (sold for 1 million ETB), and conceptual series such as “Eclipse of the Soul” and “Luminous Legacy”.

I’ve created art for cafés, construction firms, music albums, and public spaces—always with a message of hope, unity, and the strength of African women.

Artistic Vision

My practice lives at the intersection of architecture, culture, and emotional storytelling. Trained as an architect, I bring structure and space into my visual art—while drawing deeply from Ethiopian identity, women’s resilience, and cosmic symbolism.
